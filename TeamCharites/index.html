What is Net Worth?

Your net worth is an equation: every significant thing you own(assets) minus what you owe (your debts and other liabilities).
Simply put, net worth illustrates the amount in cash you would have left over after selling all your assets to pay your liabilities, providing a measure of your overall financial health and stability. Your net worth can either be negative or posititve.

Liabilities

Includes what you may owe on your assets and other debts, such as mortgage, car and bank or cooperative society loans.

Assets

Includes cash, investments, landed properties, retirement accounts, bonds and stocks, your home and other valuables such as jewelry.

Why knowing your networth matters

Having mutiple investments and income streams is a good thing, but keeping track is a major point. This helps to know your standard and help you think of how best to improve it. Compare yourself to others - track your progress as compared to the average networth for your age or your income bracket
